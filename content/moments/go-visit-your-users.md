Building products for physical operations is hard. We as builders don't experience our products the same way our users do. We can't.

I visited a fleet operations facility at 6 a.m. recently. Spent the morning with drivers, fleet managers, workshop mechanics. Amongst other things, I observed how they use our idling feature.

Vehicle idling is a high-impact feature for us. We detect when a vehicle's engine is on but it is not moving. We check if it is powering equipment. We see if the ambient temperature is out of range. If not, we flag it as wasteful idling. We think of baselines, and how much money we are saving the fleets.

At this facility, when a truck idles at a loading dock, it pumps exhaust into a building where people work all day. The air their coworkers breathe is directly affected by how well our idling detection works.

A completely different reframing from what I was attuned to. I can't not think of all the edge cases that seemed low-impact when viewed dispassionately from an objective data lens but would significantly improve lives at this facility.

Go visit your users. Watch them work (while your AI agents do the data munching).
